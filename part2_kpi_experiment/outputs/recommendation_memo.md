# Recommendation Memo

## Executive Summary

The objective of this experiment was to evaluate whether the new onboarding and activation campaign should be launched to all users.

The treatment group outperformed the control group on the primary business metric, Paid Conversion Rate, while also improving engagement and reducing conversion time.

Although some guardrail metrics worsened, the overall business impact was positive.

---

## North Star Metric

Paid Conversion Rate

The North Star Metric was selected because it directly measures the percentage of users who become paying customers and therefore has the strongest connection to business growth and revenue generation.

---

## KPI Tree Explanation

The Paid Conversion Rate is influenced by three primary drivers:

1. User Acquisition
   - Landing Page Visit Rate
   - Trial Start Rate

2. Onboarding Effectiveness
   - Onboarding Completion Rate
   - Days To Convert

3. User Engagement
   - Engagement Score
   - Revenue Per User

Guardrail metrics monitored:
- Refund Rate
- Support Ticket Rate
- Segment-Level Decline

---

## Experiment Result Summary

| Metric | Control | Treatment |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Paid Conversion Rate | 3.17% | 6.99% |
| Average Revenue Per User | 51.75 | 53.88 |
| Average Engagement Score | 57.03 | 62.93 |
| Average Days To Convert | 8.86 | 6.40 |

The treatment group demonstrated stronger overall performance across key business metrics.

---

## Hypothesis Test Interpretation

The experiment tested whether the treatment onboarding campaign improved Paid Conversion Rate.

The treatment group achieved a Paid Conversion Rate of 6.99% compared to 3.17% in the control group.

This result indicates a meaningful improvement in conversion performance and supports the effectiveness of the new onboarding experience.

---

## Guardrail Analysis

### Refund Rate
Refund requests increased slightly from 0.00% to 0.42%.

### Support Ticket Rate
Support ticket volume increased from 14.72% to 24.76%.

### Average Days To Convert
Users converted faster under the treatment experience.

### Engagement Score
User engagement improved under the treatment experience.

Overall, the treatment produced positive business outcomes but requires monitoring of support ticket volume and refund requests.

---

## Segment-Level Insights

Segment analysis was performed across:
- Region
- Device Type
- Traffic Source

The treatment group generally outperformed the control group across key segments and no major segment-level decline was observed.

---

## Final Recommendation

### Launch With Monitoring

The treatment onboarding campaign should be launched because it significantly improved Paid Conversion Rate, increased engagement, and reduced conversion time.

The company should continue monitoring refund rates and support ticket volume after launch.

---

## Risks And Limitations

- Missing values existed in some fields.
- Analysis was limited to the available experiment period.
- Long-term retention behavior was not measured.
- Future user behavior may differ from the experiment sample.

---

## Next Steps

1. Launch the treatment experience.
2. Monitor refund and support ticket trends.
3. Continue tracking Paid Conversion Rate.
4. Conduct follow-up experiments to further improve onboarding performance.
